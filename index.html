About AstroStellar

Comets are basically dusty snowballs which orbit the Sun. They are made of ices, such as water, carbon dioxide, ammonia and methane, mixed with dust. These materials came from the time when the Solar System was formed. Comets have an icy center surrounded by a large cloud of gas and dust

Hale–Bopp was a record-breaking comet. The farthest comet from the Sun discovered by amateurs, with the largest well-measured cometary nucleus, and it was visible to the naked eye for twice as long as the previous record-holder.

Encke

Comet Encke is a small comet. Its nucleus measures approximately 2.98 miles in diameter, which is about a third of the size of the object hypothesized to have led to the demise of the dinosaurs. It takes 3.30 years for Enke to orbit the sun once.

Halley

Halley's Comet or Comet Halley, officially designated 1P/Halley, is a short-period comet visible from Earth every 75–76 years. Halley is the only known short-period comet that is regularly visible to the naked eye from Earth
